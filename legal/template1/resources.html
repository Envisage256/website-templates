Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about working with our firm

To make the most of your initial consultation, please bring:

  • Relevant documents: Any contracts, correspondence, court documents, police reports, or other paperwork related to your legal matter.
  • Timeline of events: A written chronology of important dates and events.
  • List of questions: Write down any questions or concerns you want to discuss.
  • Contact information: Names, addresses, and phone numbers of other parties involved.
  • Photo identification: A valid driver's license or passport.
  • Payment method: For the consultation fee (if applicable).

If you're unsure whether a document is relevant, bring it anyway. It's better to have too much information than too little.

The duration of a legal matter varies significantly depending on the type of case, complexity, and circumstances. Here are general timeframes:

  • Simple contract review: 1-2 weeks
  • Real estate closing: 30-60 days
  • Uncontested divorce: 3-6 months
  • Contested divorce: 1-2 years
  • Personal injury case: 6 months to 2+ years
  • Criminal case: 3 months to 1+ years
  • Business litigation: 1-3+ years

During your consultation, your attorney will provide a more specific estimate based on the details of your situation.

While every case is different, most legal matters follow a general process:

  1. Initial Consultation: We meet to discuss your situation, assess your options, and determine the best strategy.
  2. Engagement: If you decide to hire us, we'll sign a retainer agreement outlining our representation, fees, and expectations.
  3. Investigation & Discovery: We gather evidence, interview witnesses, and research applicable laws.
  4. Negotiation: In many cases, we attempt to resolve matters through negotiation or mediation before going to court.
  5. Litigation (if necessary): If negotiation fails, we file court documents and represent you through the trial process.
  6. Resolution: Your case concludes through settlement, verdict, or dismissal.
  7. Post-Resolution: We ensure all terms are properly executed and answer any follow-up questions.

Your attorney will keep you informed at every stage and explain what to expect next.

We offer several fee structures depending on the type of case:

  • Hourly rates: You pay for the actual time spent on your case. Rates vary by attorney experience and case complexity.
  • Flat fees: A set fee for specific services like document preparation, simple contracts, or uncontested matters.
  • Contingency fees: Available for certain cases (typically personal injury). We only get paid if you win, usually a percentage of the recovery.
  • Retainers: An upfront deposit that covers initial work. We bill against this retainer and request replenishment as needed.

We always provide a clear fee agreement before beginning work. There are no hidden charges, and you'll receive regular billing statements.

Communication is a priority at Sterling & Associates. Here's what you can expect:

  • We return phone calls and emails within one business day.
  • You'll receive updates whenever there's significant activity in your case.
  • For active litigation, expect updates at least monthly, more frequently during critical periods.
  • You'll receive copies of all important documents filed or received.
  • You can always call or email with questions—we're here to help.

We encourage open communication. If you ever feel out of the loop, please let us know immediately.

Absolutely. Attorney-client privilege is one of the oldest and most sacred protections in our legal system.

  • Everything you tell your attorney is confidential and protected by law.
  • We cannot share your information without your express permission (with very limited exceptions required by law).
  • This protection extends to all members of our team, including paralegals and support staff.
  • Your files are stored securely, both physically and electronically.

This protection allows you to be completely honest with us, which is essential for effective representation.

While you're not legally required to have an attorney for most matters, professional legal representation often makes a significant difference:

  • Complexity: The law is complex. Attorneys spend years learning it and staying current with changes.
  • Stakes: If you're facing criminal charges, a lawsuit, or a significant financial transaction, the risks of going alone are high.
  • Objectivity: Legal matters are often emotional. An attorney provides objective, strategic thinking.
  • Procedure: Courts have strict rules. Missing a deadline or filing incorrectly can harm your case.
  • Negotiation: Attorneys are trained negotiators who can often achieve better outcomes.

If you're unsure, call us for a free 15-minute phone screening. We'll honestly tell you whether you need an attorney.

We understand that legal emergencies don't always happen during business hours.

  • Current clients: You'll receive your attorney's direct phone number and email for urgent matters.
  • After-hours answering service: Call our main number, and our answering service can reach the on-call attorney for genuine emergencies.
  • Criminal arrests: If you or a family member is arrested, call immediately. We can provide guidance and, if necessary, attend a bail hearing.

For true emergencies involving immediate danger, always call 911 first.

Legal Terms Glossary

Common legal terms explained in plain English

Plaintiff
The person or party who initiates a lawsuit by filing a complaint against another party.
Defendant
The person or party being sued or accused in a legal proceeding.
Deposition
Testimony given under oath outside of court, typically recorded and used as evidence.
Discovery
The pre-trial process where both sides exchange information and evidence relevant to the case.
Retainer
An upfront fee paid to secure an attorney's services, typically held in a trust account.
Statute of Limitations
The legal deadline by which a lawsuit must be filed. Missing this deadline can bar your claim.
